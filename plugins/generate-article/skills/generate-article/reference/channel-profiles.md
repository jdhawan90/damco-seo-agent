# Channel Profiles

Pick the row that matches the `platform` input. The "Channel focus" column is the
single biggest driver of how the article should read. Everything in
`writing-rules.md` applies to **every** channel on top of this.

The platform value maps to a folder under `Generated/`:
`SEO Articles`, `Paid Guest Blog`, `Guest Blog`, `Medium`, `LinkedIn`.

---

## SEO Articles  (on-site Damco blog)

- **Channel focus:** Topical depth and search intent come first. Cover the real
  questions a searcher for this keyword has — comprehensively, in a scannable
  structure — *while still leading with the thesis*. This is the channel where the
  "thesis vs. coverage" balance must tip toward coverage. Lead with the thesis in
  the intro, then systematically cover the intent map (see SKILL.md step 3).
- **Meta title + meta description:** REQUIRED.
- **Keyword mechanics:** Strict. Primary keyword in title, ≥1 H2, first two
  paragraphs, and conclusion. Secondary keywords for semantic coverage.
- **Mandatory sections:** Key Takeaways box near the top, FAQ (4-6 People-Also-Ask
  questions) near the end, full Sources list.
- **Stats:** ≥3 (aim 5+) inline-cited 2025-2026 stats from primary sources.
- **Voice:** Second person (you/your) throughout. Do not mix persons.
- **CTA:** Embed brand CTA URL 2-3x naturally.

## Paid Guest Blog  (external publication, paid placement)

- **Channel focus:** Thought leadership and storytelling over raw keyword coverage.
  Open with a narrative or a sharp observation. Make an argument the host's
  audience has not read many times. Still cover the core subtopics, but the prose
  carries it more than the keyword grid.
- **Meta title + meta description:** REQUIRED (host needs them).
- **Keyword mechanics:** Natural placement; do not stuff. Primary keyword in title,
  opening, conclusion, and at least one H2.
- **Mandatory sections:** FAQ recommended; Sources list required; Key Takeaways box
  recommended.
- **Stats:** ≥3 inline-cited primary-source stats; cite at the point of use.
- **Voice:** Second person, or confident practitioner voice — stay consistent.
- **CTA:** 2-3x, but lighter touch; external publications limit self-promotion.

## Guest Blog  (e.g. Medium-hosted guest post)

- **Channel focus:** Conversational and opinion-driven. Hook fast, keep paragraphs
  short, take a clear personal stance, lighter voice than a formal blog. Close on a
  point that invites discussion rather than a tidy summary.
- **Meta title + meta description:** REQUIRED.
- **Keyword mechanics:** Natural, light. Primary keyword in title + opening.
- **Mandatory sections:** Sources list required; Key Takeaways and FAQ optional but
  helpful.
- **Stats:** ≥3 inline-cited primary-source stats.
- **Voice:** First person or we-voice as a senior practitioner; stay consistent.
- **CTA:** 2-3x, natural.

## Medium

- Same as **Guest Blog** above (conversational, opinion-driven). Medium rewards a
  strong personal POV and a discussion-inviting close. Keep paragraphs short and
  give it a distinct narrative spine.

## LinkedIn  (long-form thought-leadership article — v2 bar)

- **Channel focus:** This is the most demanding bar and the model the other channels
  should aspire to. Full SEO/AEO quality applies; only meta title, meta description,
  and strict keyword *density* are relaxed (still place keywords for craft).
- **Required quality elements (all of them):**
  - Specific, cited hook in the first two lines (earn the "see more" click).
  - **Key Takeaways box** (3-6 short, liftable bullets) near the top.
  - A plain-language **definition callout** for the central term ("What is X").
  - Named real-world examples *or* clearly-framed representative scenarios with
    concrete numbers.
  - At least one firsthand "here is what we have seen" observation (EEAT).
  - A comparison table where it earns its place + a short **FAQ** (4-6 PAA questions).
  - At least one clean functional infographic (scorecard / process flow / stat band /
    comparison) in brand palette (navy #1F3864, teal #2A9D8F). See SKILL.md for how
    to embed an image; if image tooling is unavailable, render the same content as a
    formatted comparison table and note that a designed visual should replace it.
- **Stats:** ≥5 inline-cited stats, all 2024-2026, primary sources only.
- **Voice:** Confident first-person / we-voice as Damco's senior practitioner.
- **Listicles ("Top N…"):** see `listicle-rules.md` — applies to any channel but is
  spelled out in the LinkedIn brief.
- **CTA:** In the close and 1-2 other natural spots. Close with a POV or a direct
  question to the reader.

## Fixed brief defaults (all channels unless overridden)

- **Brand name:** Damco Solutions
- **Target audience:** CIOs, CTOs, IT decision-makers (unless the brief says otherwise)
- **Word count:** 2000-2500. Never exceed 2500. Quality over volume; never pad.
- **Grammar:** US English, Chicago Manual of Style.
- **Known CTA URLs by service** (confirm against the brief / title intent):
  - Healthcare app development: `https://www.damcogroup.com/healthcare/healthcare-app-development`
  - Microsoft Dynamics 365: `https://www.damcogroup.com/microsoft-dynamics-365-services`
  - AI consulting: `https://www.damcogroup.com/ai-consulting-services`
  - Salesforce / general: confirm with the user; Salesforce paid-blog brief used `https://achieva.ai/`
  - If unsure, ask the user for the exact CTA URL — never guess a slug.
